  - q: 'Drum vs wing pulley — when?'
    a: 'Drum pulleys are the default for drive and head positions; wing pulleys shed material at the tail where fines build up and would otherwise get trapped between belt and pulley. Many operations run drum at the head, wing at the tail.'
  - q: 'Do you supply lagging?'
    a: 'Yes — lagged pulleys in the common rubber and ceramic patterns. Lagging protects the shell and keeps drive traction in wet conditions; tell us your application and we will recommend a pattern.'

The right pulley — correctly lagged — protects your belt and your uptime.
HardRok supplies head, tail, drive, and take-up pulleys in drum and wing
styles, bare or lagged, sized to your shaft, bearing, and belt. Send your
pulley dimensions or a photo, and we'll quote a match.

We stock and source **PCI (ProCal Innovations)** wing and drum pulleys —
including Eradicator® wing pulleys that shed recirculated material faster
than traditional wings, cutting belt wear and vibration. Standard builds use
C1045 steel shafts (4140 alloy for high-stress duty), spherical roller
bearings suited to the shock loads and minor misalignment of aggregate
service, and XT®-style bushing interfaces.
